Sunflower (Helianthus annuus)

This flower called Sunflower because of it's behavior to follow a sun (sun follower), which this behavior called heliotropism.

Their buds and blossoms start the day facing east and then follow the sun until it sets in the west. But, when the sunflower are undergoing seed production, mature flower heads become heavier and stiffer than usual and remain facing east for the rest of the day.
